Are you one of the people who struggle to stay away from work when on vacation or switch off in the evenings? I've known a couple of those and always wondered how and why they do it. For me, it is a matter of principle: when I'm off, I'm off and if you need me, I'm not here.

I had a mini-vacation of a couple of days that I spent building our tiny country house (which is a whole other post in itself). During those days, I was rarely found behind a screen. Instead, I was sanding and plastering walls, filling cracks with acrylic, emptying an old sauna, and doing various other tasks.

Taking breaks is important because...

Best ideas come when our brain has the chance to be bored: shower, waiting in line, lounging

Farmers are the happiest
